How would you describe your taste in music?

My playlists are pretty eclectic. I enjoy artists across various genres—from SZA and Kendrick Lamar to Atarashii Gakko, as well as classics like Van Morrison and The Fugees. There's something about having diverse musical influences that helps keep my thinking flexible and often sparks creativity in unexpected ways.

What video games have captured your attention lately?

Star Citizen is my main go-to—I'm drawn to its expansive sci-fi sandbox environment. Different games serve different purposes for me: FPS titles when I need to unwind, Path of Exile 2 when I want to zone out, and games like Baldur's Gate 3 and Kingdom Come: Deliverance for their immersive worlds. Gaming offers a nice complement to technical work—it's problem-solving in entirely different contexts.

What's your ideal way to spend a weekend?

I value balance in my downtime. A good weekend involves relaxed mornings with coffee, catching up on shows or games, and exploring local spots with my fiancé. I've found that having this clear separation between work and leisure helps me return to projects with renewed focus and perspective.

What's something seemingly trivial you've thought deeply about?

The logic behind how we define "afternoon." The word literally means "after noon," yet we typically use it to describe times between 1pm and 6pm. This attention to linguistic precision might seem minor, but it reflects the same mindset that makes for effective technical communication—questioning assumptions and seeking clarity in how we express ideas.

How do you recharge when you're feeling drained?

I find that occasionally disconnecting from technology is essential. Taking day trips with my fiancé to explore new places helps reset my thinking. This change of environment improves my focus and creativity when I return to work—maintaining this balance contributes significantly to sustained productivity and fresh perspectives.
