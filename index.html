Frequently Asked Questions

Great documentation anticipates questions. Great technical communicators answer them.

What types of documentation do you specialize in?

I specialize in creating user manuals, product guides, knowledge base articles, and training materials that transform complex technical concepts into clear, actionable content. My experience spans both technical documentation and instructional design, with particular strengths in onboarding materials, troubleshooting guides, and process documentation.

Whether it's creating step-by-step guides for end-users or developing comprehensive technical references for internal teams, I focus on matching the documentation style to the audience's needs and knowledge level.

How do you approach a new documentation project?

My process begins with stakeholder interviews and audience analysis to understand the specific needs and knowledge gaps. I then collaborate with subject matter experts to gather technical information, develop a content strategy, and create a documentation plan.

For complex projects, I develop a structured information architecture before drafting content, ensuring consistency and scalability. Each deliverable undergoes technical review, user testing, and quality assurance before publication. Throughout the process, I maintain a focus on user needs, readability, and technical accuracy.

What's your availability for new opportunities?

I'm currently open to new full-time opportunities in instructional design and technical writing roles. I can start a new position with two weeks' notice.

I'm open to remote, hybrid, or on-site positions in the greater Boston area. For the right opportunity, I'm also open to relocation. I'm particularly interested in roles where I can leverage my instructional design experience while growing my technical writing skills.

How do you keep documentation updated with rapidly changing products?

Maintaining current documentation in fast-paced environments requires a systematic approach. I implement documentation as code principles where appropriate, maintain close relationships with development and product teams, and establish clear version control practices.

I also believe in creating modular content that can be updated efficiently, implementing regular review cycles, and using analytics to identify documentation areas that need attention. For particularly dynamic products, I develop templates and reusable components that streamline updates while maintaining consistency.

Can you describe your experience transitioning technical content between systems?

I've led several content migration projects, including the transition of a knowledge base with over 500 articles from a legacy system to Confluence. This process involved content auditing, information architecture restructuring, metadata standardization, and establishing new governance processes.

The key to successful content migration is balancing technical requirements with user experience considerations. I believe in preserving what works, improving what doesn't, and ensuring the new system better serves both content creators and end users. My approach typically includes thorough planning, stakeholder alignment, content transformation, and post-migration optimization.
