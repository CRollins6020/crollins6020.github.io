What's something seemingly trivial you've thought deeply about?

The logic behind how we define 'afternoon.' If you break down the word, it literally means 'after noon,' yet we're taught it's the time between 1pm and 6pm. But here's my thing - I think 'afternoon' really includes noon itself, regardless of what the name implies. Think about it: When do people eat lunch? In the morning? No. In the evening? No. In the afternoon? Yes. And at what time? NOON! So we're all sitting there at 12:00 having our 'afternoon lunch' at a time that's technically not afternoon. Maybe that's why documentation is always delivered 'this afternoon'—nobody actually knows when that is!

How do you recharge when you're feeling drained?

I need to fully disconnect from technology sometimes. Taking a day trip, just driving somewhere new, exploring a bit, finding a decent place to eat. Nothing elaborate or Instagram-worthy, just a change of scenery without emails or notifications.
